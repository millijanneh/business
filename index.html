Tours

SOUTH GAMBIA EXPERIENCE

Join me on this tour to see the most visited places in one day. Pick up is at 8:30am and we drive to crocodile pool in bakau, monkey park, serrekunda market (including the batik factory), lamin lodge or brikama craft market, we will then head for lunch, swimming in the sea and Sundays there is a wrestling competition you wouldn‚Äôt want to miss, then our last stop on our back to the hotel is the biggest fishing market in the Gambia (Tanji fish market).

We can also make a visit to a local school or a local family in the village if it is not on a Sunday.

Please contact me for more information about the 4x4 experience.

SENEGAL (FATHALA) WILDLIFE RESERVE

The Fathala park is located to the north of Senegal. You will be pick up early morning to drive to Banjul, the capital of the Gambia. Where you will join the public ferry to barra (north bank) and experience the bustle of the people at the crossing point. After 45 minutes on the ferry you join a 4x4 drive that will now head to the border for passport checking then drive to the park. In the park you can see animals like Zebras, Giraffes, Antelopes, buffaloes, rhinos, birds, crocodiles and so on. After the safari we drive back to barra for a lunch then join the ferry back to Banjul and the hotel.

GEORGETOWN OVERNIGHT

You will be pick up early morning and drive through the south bank of the Gambia to farafenni. Visit the stone circle of Wassu and learn what is known about them before cruising further down the river Gambia where you might catch sight of wild hippos, crocodiles, baboons and chimpanzees. For those who like birds there will be chances to see many birds. We will then spend the night at the Jangjang-bureh camp. The camp has cozy rooms with running water, toilets and beds with mosquito nets.

On day 2 we leave the camp after breakfast to visit Georgetown. You will see the local market as well as the old slave house. Lunch at Tendaba camp and We'll be back home at around 7 pm.

ROOTS JAMES ISLAND

This is a very popular trip to the home of Kunta Kinteh. We start at 8:00 in the morning and head for the Banjul ferry terminal where we will cross the river to Barra. From there we will go to visit James Island, the infamous center for slaves some 200 years ago. The island is a part of UNESCO's world heritage. Lunch and drinks included.

MAKASUTU

This is a trip to the Makasutu park just 45 minutes away. On the tour we'll see a lot of baboons and different birds. We will meet a fortune teller and it's also possible to make a boat trip on the Gambia river for 45 minutes and then back to the park.

We also visit the palm wine tapper where you will be able to try the palm wine called jungle juice.
